## Assignment due at the start of class

### Part 1: Finish your project

Complete your final project or, if it's not yet fully finished, reach a major milestone.

### Part 2: Final presentation

This is our last class together. You'll share what you made and what you
  learned about your own process. Each of you will have ~7 minutes total: about
  3 minutes to present, with the rest for dialogue.

Prepare a 3-minute presentation about your project and your process:

- As with our guest feedback class, we'll share a slide deck to keep things
  moving.
- [Please add 3 slides to this shared
  deck](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1MTQuXirU5UdTRJ9A9C6aguSpRUPSZLf73P0HAYH9jvw/edit?slide=id.g39a41466ea1_0_0#slide=id.g39a41466ea1_0_0).
- Keep text minimal. Slides should support what you say, not replace it.
- **Slide 1:** Introduce your project. Quickly remind us: what are you making
  and who is it for? Include the following:
  - A title
  - 1-2 sentence description
  - ~1 min demo video (make sure video is shared for public viewing), OR an
    image plus plan to demo live if your project is hosted online
- **Slide 2:** Share one defining moment in making this project: a challenge,
  a breakthrough, or a pivot. What did you have to figure out? Which specific
  resources or strategies got you through it?
  - Include a link or screenshot/image of at least one resource you used: a
    Resident, an AI chat, documentation, a tutorial, a sketch, a message thread, etc. Crop or blur anything you'd rather keep private.
  - Here's some vocabulary to draw from when naming the ways(s) a resource
    helped you. Use what fits:
    - Outsourcing • Did something you didn't know how to do
    - Tutorial • Gave step-by-step instructions
    - Insight • Helped you understand something hard to grasp
    - Problem-solving • Helped you work out a solution to a hard problem
    - Coaching • Helped you work through a problem on your own
    - Brainstorming • Helped you come up with an idea
    - Critique / Feedback • Gave you feedback on an idea or project
    - Clarifying • Helped you crystallize an idea
    - Discovery • Helped you evolve or deepen an idea
    - Documentation • Summarized something for you to reference
- **Slide 3:** Reflect on what you learned about your approach to coding.
  What's next for this project, or what will you carry into the next one?
