## Assignment

We are all moving into project development mode. No matter where you are in your process, the goal this week is the same: **build something you can share with the class when we meet next.**

For some of you, this means getting a first version off the ground. For others, it means pushing into the next phase of work you've already started. Either way, the aim is to arrive with something visible, testable, or demonstrable, not polished, not finished, just *shareable*.

### Part 1: Set Your Target

Before you start building, get specific about what you're working toward. Answer these three questions:

1. **What is the one thing you want to have visible and/or working by next class?**
    - If you're starting a new project, this is your [Hello
      World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%22Hello,_World!%22_program): the
      smallest, simplest version of your idea that you can actually build.
      [Here's an example.](https://ellennicklesdemo.github.io/helloworld/)
    - If you're continuing an existing project, this is your next milestone: the piece that moves your project forward in a meaningful way.
2. **What does "finished enough to share" look like?** Not polished, not perfect. 
3. **What do you need to figure out to get there?** Name the unknowns. These might be technical, conceptual, or resource-related.

If you find it helpful, you can use the [SMART criteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SMART_criteria) (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) to structure your goal. [Here's a guide](https://www.ucop.edu/local-human-resources/_files/performance-appraisal/How%20to%20write%20SMART%20Goals%20v2.pdf) (PDF).

### Part 2: Build It

Using your target as a guide, start making. Your first attempt does not need to be polished, perfect, or even fully functional. Think of Anne Lamott's "[Shitty First Drafts](https://wrd.as.uky.edu/sites/default/files/1-Shitty%20First%20Drafts.pdf)" from *Bird by Bird*: just get something down so you have something to build on.

What this looks like depends on your project and goals:

#### If your goal is product-oriented (I want to make X or a series of Xs)

- Starting a new project? Make one bare-bones piece of functionality.
- Making a custom website? Publish a [Hello World site](https://ellennicklesdemo.github.io/helloworld/) on a static hosting service such as [GitHub Pages](https://github.com/ellennickles/code-your-way-s24/blob/main/version-control-guides/tips-and-tricks.md#github-pages), [Netlify](https://www.netlify.com/), etc.
- Returning to a previous project? Focus on one area this week. Does it need a major refactor? Expanded functionality? Graphic design updates? Deployment to a live environment? [Web accessibility](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/Accessibility) improvements?
- Working on a series of related code-driven pieces? Create at least one or more
 pieces to share based on your project.

#### If your goal is process-oriented (I want to learn, practice, or improve X)

- Learning a new library, tool, or technique? Create at least one experiment to apply your understanding and start building momentum.
- Improving a previously-learned skill? Show your progress by:
    - Re-making something you built earlier from scratch
    - Refactoring an old project to improve structure and efficiency
    - Recording a short walkthrough explaining how a piece of code works

#### If you've been building for weeks and have a working version already

Push into the area that feels least resolved. What's the part you've been avoiding or aren't sure how to approach? That's probably your most valuable next step.

#### Feeling stuck?

Lower the bar. What's the absolute smallest version you can create? A single function? A basic animation? A title screen? Start somewhere.

### Part 3: Reflect and Document

Reflect on your process and progress this week. In your documentation, address:

- What puzzle(s) did you set out to solve?
- What did you try? What worked? What didn't?
- Did you run into unexpected challenges or breakthroughs?
- Do you need to adjust your approach, timeline, or resources? Revisit your
  proposal. Does anything need to change?
- If you used AI tools (Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) in your process this
  week, briefly describe:
  - One moment where AI helped
  - One moment where it didn't or fell short
  - If you chose not to use AI, note why.
  - We'll talk more about this in class.

Include links to your code, documentation, media, etc. Remember to cite all references and source materials.
